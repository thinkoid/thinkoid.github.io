libccittfax

The CCITT T.4 and T.6 recommendations were written for fax machines: terminal sizes, line lengths in millimetres, signal timing, return-to-control. PDF borrows the codec and discards the machine — what remains is a bit-serial compression scheme embedded in a byte-oriented stream, with its own knobs for alignment, end-of-line handling and padding.

libccittfax implements exactly that remainder: a clean-room Group 3 / Group 4 decoder shaped the way PDF’s CCITTFaxDecode filter needs it, small enough to sit behind a plain C API or a C++ boost::iostreams filter. It is the fax codec behind ypdf.

The project has a history of abandonment: I wrote the one-dimensional Group 3 codec, lost steam and interest, and with them all hope of ever finding the time and energy to finish. A few attempts were made at completing it with the help of LLMs — notably ChatGPT and DeepSeek — but it was Claude that delivered the code quality that finished the work.
