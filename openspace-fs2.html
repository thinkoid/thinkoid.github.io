openspace-fs2

openspace-fs2 is a Linux port of the original 1998 FreeSpace 2 engine — NOT the community fork, but Volition’s pristine 2002 CVS source. It remains deliberately retail: the original campaign and scripting language, single-player only, with no community extensions. fs2open is kept nearby as a reference and “fix-mine,” never as a foundation.

The game runs at 1024×768 through both its revived 8-bit software rasterizer and OpenGL backend, matched frame for frame. Removing multiplayer and obsolete subsystems cut the codebase from roughly 404,000 lines to 146,000. Examining some 2,500 community-fork commits yielded 20 genuine retail fixes — and revealed that several famous “Volition bugs” were actually regressions introduced by the fork.

But the port is a means, not the end: understanding is the deliverable, and working code is the proof. The SEXP scripting reader was carved out and verified across 24,504 mission trees; the POF loader across all 176 retail models. Alongside them sits an x87-era rasterizer whose float-bit tricks silently change under SSE2 — a preserved specimen from the transition to GPU rendering. pofview is the smaller sibling of this work, approaching the same POF format from the viewer side.
