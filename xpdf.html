xpdf

Xpdf 3.04 — the last of the Motif line — is mid-1990s C++: hand-rolled containers, bespoke codecs, its own string class. It is also a remarkably robust PDF implementation, hardened by two decades of malformed documents in the wild. I started this fork strictly to learn PDF processing — or rather, to glimpse into others’ attempts at it.

Slowly carving at the edges, developing my own parser (ypdf) in parallel, I developed a sense of where the project was supposed to go: everything non-parsing, non-modeling dropped to the side, and it ended in the shape it holds today — a parser with a document model. The modernization is incremental C++17, without spending the accumulated robustness: the codec family is delegated to mainstream libraries (and libfaxtiff for the fax filters), the stream machinery is rebuilt on boost::iostreams, and every step is pinned by a regression oracle against the stock build over a large corpus.

Software archaeology in the literal sense: the value of old code is the knowledge buried in it, and the point of the dig is to carry the knowledge forward, not the artifacts.
