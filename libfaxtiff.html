libfaxtiff

Sam Leffler’s CCITT fax codec has shipped inside libtiff since the early 1990s — one of the most battle-tested Group 3 / Group 4 implementations in existence, and thoroughly entangled with the TIFF container machinery around it. libfaxtiff is that codec, carved out: lifted from libtiff, freed of the file format, and wrapped behind a tiny buffer-in / buffer-out C API.

It compresses and decompresses raw 1-bit rasters in the T.4 and T.6 variants (G3 one- and two-dimensional, G4), depends on nothing, and ships with a pair of command-line tools for round-tripping images. It is the fax codec behind xpdf. Extraction as preservation: the codec keeps working long after the scaffolding it grew in has become the hard part.
